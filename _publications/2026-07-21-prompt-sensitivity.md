Large language models exhibit notable sensitivity to prompt formulations, where semantically equivalent prompts can yield substantially different outputs. This paper presents an interaction-based framework to systematically evaluate and explain prompt sensitivity. By leveraging Shapley interactions, we decompose the contributions of individual tokens and their interactions to model outputs, providing fine-grained explanations for why certain prompt variations lead to different responses. Our framework introduces quantitative metrics for measuring prompt sensitivity at both the token and interaction levels, enabling researchers to identify which prompt components are most influential and how they interact. Experiments across diverse tasks and models demonstrate that our approach effectively captures and explains prompt sensitivity patterns, offering practical guidance for prompt engineering and robustness improvement.
